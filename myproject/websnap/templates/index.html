By Hot Thai Kitchen

🌶️ Tom Yum(ต้มยำ) is a Thai masterpiece! This hot and sour soup bursts with lemongrass, galangal, and makrut lime leaves, delivering a delightful blend of **spicy, sour, salty**, and **sweet** flavors.

🍤 Shrimp takes the spotlight in this Thai favorite, but you can experiment with other proteins. It's a versatile dish enjoyed across Thailand, from street stalls to high-end restaurants. Tom Yum has even inspired unique variations like tom yum spaghetti 🍝 and pizza 🍕!

🌟 Don't miss the chance to experience the heart and soul of Thai cuisine through Tom Yum. Dive into its captivating flavors and rich culinary culture.

Tom Kha Gai - ต้มข่าไก่

By Hot Thai Kitchen

❤️ If you're not in the mood for spicy, no problem! Here's something comforting that feels like a cuddle in a bowl. Tom kha gai is a dish that's always included in Thai cooking classes and is known for wowing people with its unique flavors.

💡What is Tom Kha Gai? Tom kha gai, sometimes spelled tom ka gai, is one of the most popular Thai soups. "Tom" means to boil, "kha" means galangal, and "gai" means chicken. So it's a chicken soup with the main flavors coming from galangal and coconut milk.

🍽️ Tom kha gai is a versatile dish perfect for weeknight home cooking. While chicken is the traditional choice, you can use other proteins like fish or seafood. It's a crowd-pleaser, gluten-free, not too spicy, and can even be made vegan with some modifications.

Pad Kra Pao - ผัดกะเพรา

By Hot Thai Kitchen

🍳 Pad Kra Pao (ผัดกะเพรา) is a beloved Thai stir-fry dish that's known by many spellings, such as pad krapow or pad kra pao. It's a Thai street food favorite and an explosion of flavors with the aroma of holy basil.

🌿 Traditionally, it's made with holy basil, but if you can't find it, Italian basil or Thai basil can do the trick. Don't forget the crispy fried egg on top—it's a game-changer!

🥢 In Thailand, it's like our fast food—quick, easy, and available everywhere. You can have it with various proteins like chicken, pork, beef, seafood, or go vegetarian with tofu and mushrooms.

👉 Serve it over rice with a fried egg for the classic combo known as "kao pad gaprao gai kai dao." The creamy egg balances out the spicy stir-fry perfectly!

Som Tum - ส้มตำ

By Hot Thai Kitchen

🍳 Som Tum - ส้มตำ is a beloved Thai salad that's known for its unique combination of flavors. This green papaya salad is a perfect side dish to complement your main course. It's crunchy, tangy, and has just the right amount of heat from chilies.

💡 What is Som Tum? Som Tum, also known as Som Tam, is made with green papaya, fish sauce, palm sugar, tamarind paste, and fresh lime juice. It's a refreshing salad that's popular in Thailand and enjoyed worldwide.

🌶️ You can adjust the level of spiciness to your liking by adding more or fewer chilies. It pairs wonderfully with sticky rice or Thai coconut rice, making it a complete and healthy meal.

🌟 In this recipe, we provide you with lots of tips and tricks to master this classic Thai salad. It's a healthy, gluten-free dish that you'll love indulging in.

🌟 Discover the Secrets of Pad Thai

Pad thai, Thailand's beloved stir-fried noodle dish, is a culinary adventure. However, mastering traditional pad thai, with its finicky rice noodles, can be challenging for beginners.

🍜 Introducing Glass Noodles

Laab Gai - ลาบไก่

By Hot Thai Kitchen

Laab Gai is a northeastern Thai salad made traditionally with ground meat. It's tossed in a spicy dressing made with fish sauce, lime juice, and toasted rice powder that adds a wonderful nutty flavor. This chicken laab is just an example, and as has been mentioned many times, "one can laab anything."

🦀 Crab Fried Rice - ข้าวผัดปู

By Hot Thai Kitchen

🌟 Dive into Thai Fried Rice Bliss

Thai fried rice may seem deceptively simple, but let me tell you, it's a flavor explosion that surprises me every time. The crab version, in particular, reigns supreme in Thailand (and in my heart). What makes it even better is that it's incredibly easy, especially for the protein part—no hassle there. Feel free to swap crab for shrimp if you prefer. Plus, you don't need day-old rice for this dish.

🍤 The King of Thai Fried Rice

Arguably the most beloved fried rice in Thailand, crab fried rice boasts such popularity that there are eateries in Thailand solely dedicated to perfecting this dish. As a child growing up in Thailand, this was my go-to comfort food. Simple, delicious, and fuss-free, this dish skips the vegetables, making it a kid's dream.

🦐 Choosing the Right Crab

Quality matters when it comes to crab. Avoid cheap canned crab meat that's more brine than flavor. Opt for generous, succulent crab chunks from a reputable seafood counter. Since this dish is all about the crab's flavor, don't skimp on quality. In Thailand, we use sweet and succulent blue crab meat. If you can find lump blue crab meat, consider yourself a winner. In my Canadian West Coast home, I work with Dungeness crab, which, while not as sweet, still delivers fantastic results when fresh.

🍚 Tips for Perfectly Fluffy Rice

MYTH: You need cold, day-old rice from the fridge to make great fried rice. Wrong! After years in restaurant kitchens, I've learned their secret: well-cooked rice in an uncrowded pan. That's it! Many folks start with overly wet rice and attempt to cook it all in one crowded pan, leading to mushy results.

🥭 Mango and Sticky Rice - ข้าวเหนียวมะม่วง

By Hot Thai Kitchen

🌟 Thai Dessert Delight

Thai desserts often don't get the recognition they deserve. But let me tell you, Thai people adore their sweets, and if you could only savor one Thai dessert in your lifetime, let it be this one. When mangoes ripen, becoming sweet and abundant, there are few pairings as heavenly as ripe mangoes and our sweet coconut sticky rice.

🍦 Versatility in Taste

When ripe mangoes are scarce, don't fret. Prepare the sticky rice (also known as glutinous rice) and serve it with ice cream, like this creamy blender mango ice cream. Or, if you're inclined to stay traditional, try a 4-ingredient street-style Thai coconut ice cream.

🥭 Mango Sticky Rice Magic

Mango sticky rice, known as ข้าวเหนียวมะม่วง in Thai, is perhaps the most renowned Thai dessert, and it's easy to see why. If you've never imagined a combination of fruit and rice, prepare to be amazed. The sweet, sticky rice wonderfully complements the sweet and juicy fresh mangoes. This delightful dessert is beloved worldwide and could be considered Thailand's national dessert!

🍚 Essential Ingredients

This mango sticky rice recipe comprises a few essential components, each with straightforward ingredients.

  • Sweet Coconut Sticky Rice: Thai white glutinous rice, coconut milk (choose high-quality coconut milk), salt, granulated sugar (used to maintain the white color and clean flavor).
  • Salted Coconut Sauce: Coconut milk, salt, rice flour or cornstarch (to thicken the sauce and keep it on the rice).
  • The Mangoes and Topping: Shelled, split mung beans (for crispy mung bean topping), sweet, ripe mangoes (use sweet, juicy mangoes like Manila or ataulfo mangoes), toasted sesame seeds can be substituted for mung beans if preferred.
